I started my African safari in Hoedspruit in South Africa and continued to the Beitbridge border crossing, a 437 kilometer stint that required just one precautionary break because of rainy weather. The next day, I crossed Limpopo River from Beitbridge, through Masvingo, and Great Zimbabwe, where I was able to check out some of that ancient city's many charms in the flesh. Finally, I finished my African journey in Zimbabwe's capital, Harare. Along the way, I was able to spend some time with Venensia Mukarati, who 'with her daughter Tino' operates 160 Hydro Farms that use hydroponics to allow crops to grow without soil, consume 90 percent less water, and avoid weeds, pests, and diseases.
